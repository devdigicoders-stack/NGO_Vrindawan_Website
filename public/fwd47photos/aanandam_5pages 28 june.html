Origin Story

It Began on a Stage — And Grew Into a Home

For several years before Aanandam existed, our founder Niraj Gera would do something unusual. He would walk into old age homes across Delhi — not with forms or inspections, but with music, energy and a camera. He would sit beside elders who hadn't been visited in months, listen to their stories, and then ask them something nobody had asked in a long time: "What are you good at?"

The answers were extraordinary. A man who had spent 40 years as a classical singer. A woman who had danced at weddings across Rajasthan. A retired teacher who still composed poetry in his room every morning. A former tailor who could sew anything from memory. All of them living in silence — their talents folded away like old letters, unseen.

Niraj's response was to create Oldies Got Talent — an annual event where residents from old age homes across Delhi were given a real stage, a real audience, and a real performance. Singers sang. Dancers danced. Poets recited. One grandmotherly woman in her eighties walked the ramp to a standing ovation she will carry to her last day.

"But what moved me most wasn't the performance. It was the three months before it — the weeks of rehearsal, the anticipation, the laughter in practice sessions, the way residents who had stopped caring about tomorrow suddenly had something to wake up for. That was the real medicine."— Niraj Gera, Founder, Humanify Foundation

Niraj began to imagine something: a home where that feeling wasn't annual, but daily. Where yoga began each morning not as exercise but as intention. Where bhajans rose each evening not as routine but as belonging. Where residents didn't just eat and sleep — but sang, laughed, rehearsed, created, contributed, and mattered.

In 2021, that imagination became Aanandam Vridhashram.

Located in Karala Village, near Rohini, Delhi — Aanandam is a 100% free old age home for abandoned, neglected and homeless senior citizens, built around one belief: that the last chapter of a person's life deserves to be the most dignified, most joyful, most meaningful of all.

On Our Approach to Care — An Honest Note

We are a community-funded, community-powered home. Our medical support currently operates through regular volunteer health camps and doctor visits. For hospitalisation or specialist care, we rely on government facilities and our growing network of medical volunteers. We do not over-promise — because the elders who trust us deserve honesty.

We are actively seeking hospital and diagnostic partners (X-ray, pathology, physiotherapy) to bring better healthcare to our residents. If your institution can help — please reach out to us.

Developer Note — Auto-Recurring Donation Framework

Making Monthly Donations Happen Automatically

The key to sustainable monthly giving is friction-free setup. Here is the complete technical and copy framework to make recurring donations work without reminders:

🔧 Technical Setup (for developer)

  • Razorpay Recurring: Integrate Razorpay's Subscription API. It handles UPI AutoPay (via UPI mandate), card auto-debit (via recurring card setup), and net banking ECS. The donor sets up once — Razorpay auto-charges on the same date every month.
  • UPI AutoPay: Donor approves a mandate on any UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm) — charges happen silently on the 1st of each month (or chosen date). No OTP needed for subsequent charges below ₹15,000.
  • WhatsApp Receipt: Integrate Razorpay webhook → WhatsApp API (via Interakt or Wati) to auto-send a receipt on every successful charge. Donor gets proof without chasing you.
  • Failure handling: If a charge fails (low balance etc.), Razorpay retries automatically 3× before marking as failed and notifying the donor via email/SMS.
  • Cancel anytime: Donor can cancel from their UPI app or Razorpay dashboard. Prominently state this — it actually increases sign-up rates.
